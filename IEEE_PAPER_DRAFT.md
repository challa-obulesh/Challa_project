# Real-Time Edge Autonomous Navigation via Direct Pixel-Centroid Kinematic Translation using SegFormer and YOLOv8
**Draft for IEEE Paper Submission**

## Abstract
Autonomous navigation for unmapped environments traditionally relies on computationally heavy Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) or complex pathfinding algorithms like A*. This paper presents a novel, lightweight sensor-fusion architecture designed explicitly for resource-constrained edge computing devices, specifically the NVIDIA Jetson AGX Orin. By combining SegFormer-B0 for semantic segmentation and YOLOv8 for object detection, our system eliminates the need for geometric pathfinding. Instead, it introduces a novel direct pixel-centroid translation method that calculates safe drivable paths in real-time and converts them directly into kinematic actions. Coupled with TensorRT acceleration and asynchronous multithreading, the proposed system achieves highly stable autonomous driving decision-making at >15 Frames Per Second (FPS) with an end-to-end latency under 80ms.

## 1. Introduction
Deploying autonomous navigation systems on edge hardware presents a strict trade-off between perception accuracy and inference latency. State-of-the-art vision models typically consume significant computational overhead, leaving little room for path planning algorithms. This paper introduces an architecture that removes traditional mapping phases in favor of immediate, pixel-level decision-making.

## 2. Proposed Methodology

### A. Novel Pixel-Centroid Kinematic Translation
The core novelty of this research is the bypass of traditional cost-map and spline-based path generation. Rather than projecting points into 3D space, the system operates purely on 2D semantic masks. 
A "forward-lookahead" band is dynamically monitored at the bottom 40% of the camera frame. The system computes the raw image moment (centroid) of the drivable surface pixels (e.g., road, terrain) within this band. The lateral offset of this centroid from the camera's center axis is directly translated into steering commands (`ACTION: HARD LEFT`, `ACTION: FORWARD`, etc.) using an arctangent function. A low-pass filter (α = 0.10) smooths the output to mimic physically realistic vehicle kinematics.

### B. Obstacle and Semantic Fusion
To avoid obstacles, bounding boxes generated by YOLOv8n are artificially inflated based on safety tolerances and converted into a Boolean obstacle mask. This mask is subtracted from SegFormer's drivable surface mask. If the road centroid is obstructed, or if the drivable surface pixel count drops below a strict threshold (15,000 pixels), the system immediately halts the kinematic action and triggers a `STOP` or `CAUTION` state.

### C. Traffic Signal and Sign Board Parsing
Regulatory adherence is integrated without additional neural networks. Bounding boxes classified as "traffic light" by YOLO are cropped, converted to HSV color space, and statistically thresholded to identify RED, GREEN, or YELLOW states. Simultaneously, SegFormer's 'traffic sign' class contours are extracted to identify signboards, ensuring robust regulatory context.

## 3. Implementation and Edge Optimizations
To achieve real-time capability on the NVIDIA Jetson AGX Orin, several optimizations were implemented:
- **TensorRT Acceleration:** Both the SegFormer-B0 and YOLOv8n models were exported to TensorRT (`.engine`), executing highly optimized CUDA kernels natively on the Ampere GPU.
- **Concurrent Inference:** Python's `ThreadPoolExecutor` is utilized to decouple the two models, allowing YOLO and SegFormer to execute in parallel across the frame.
- **Asynchronous I/O Pipeline:** Both camera capture and video encoding are isolated to background threads with queue buffers. This entirely prevents hard-disk writes and camera polling limits from bottlenecking the neural network inference loops.
- **Pre-computed Look-up Tables (LUTs):** Color mapping and traversable-class identification operate on `O(1)` Numpy arrays rather than iterative conditional loops.

## 4. Experimental Results
The system was evaluated in real-world recorded environments involving complex obstacles, pedestrians, and traffic signals.
- **Throughput:** The asynchronous pipeline successfully processes frames at **>15 FPS** continuously on the Jetson AGX Orin.
- **Latency:** Parallel inference cycles evaluate the frame in **~60-80ms**.
- **Accuracy:** The dynamic thresholding successfully prevented false-positive braking from distant objects. A pedestrian must occupy >8% of the camera frame (indicating immediate proximity) to trigger a hard stop, ensuring smooth forward progression otherwise.

## 5. Conclusion
This paper demonstrates that by abandoning geometric pathfinding in favor of direct pixel-centroid translation, heavy multi-model perception stacks can be successfully run on edge devices for autonomous navigation. The proposed system provides a robust, low-latency framework for robotics and autonomous vehicle applications operating in unstructured environments.
