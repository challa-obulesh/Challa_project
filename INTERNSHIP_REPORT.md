# Internship Report: Real-Time Semantic Segmentation for Autonomous Navigation on NVIDIA Jetson

## Project Overview
The objective of this project was to develop a robust, vision-based navigation pipeline capable of understanding complex environments. The project evolved from a basic semantic road-visualizer into a comprehensive, risk-aware autonomous driving system (V5).

## System Architecture (V5)
The final architecture integrates two complementary deep learning models:
1. **Semantic Segmentation (NVIDIA SegFormer)**: Classifies every pixel into one of 19 Cityscapes classes, providing a foundational understanding of static environments (roads, sidewalks, buildings, vegetation). SegFormer is also used to extract and highlight traffic sign boards.
2. **Object Detection (YOLOv8)**: Provides real-time detection and tracking of dynamic obstacles (pedestrians, vehicles, cyclists) and explicitly detects traffic lights to classify their current states (RED/GREEN/YELLOW).

## Key Innovations

### 1. Pure Pixel-Level Navigation
Instead of computationally expensive A* pathfinding or complex risk maps, the system uses a highly optimized pure pixel-level approach. By evaluating a "lookahead band", the pipeline directly counts traversable pixels (road, sidewalk) to deduce path viability. A sudden drop in traversable pixels automatically shifts the robot into a `CAUTION` or `STOP` state.

### 2. Calibrated Obstacle Avoidance
The system merges SegFormer's traversable space with a boolean obstacle mask generated by YOLOv8. Obstacles are dynamically inflated. To prevent jitter and false-positive lockups, pedestrians only trigger a full stop if their bounding box area exceeds 3% of the frame, ensuring the robot doesn't halt for distant figures. 

### 3. Median Centroid Steering
The steering angle is calculated by locating the median X-coordinate of all clear, traversable pixels in the lookahead band. This provides a natural, smooth path that avoids obstacles and hugs the center of the available free space. The raw angle is processed through a low-pass filter (alpha=0.20) for jitter-free steering.

### 4. Traffic Signal and Sign Awareness
The system uses color-space thresholding (HSV) on the cropped bounding boxes of traffic lights detected by YOLOv8 to actively determine their state (RED, GREEN, YELLOW). Simultaneously, it extracts regions classified as traffic signs (class 7) by SegFormer, providing the groundwork for compliance with traffic regulations.

## Conclusion
The V5 pipeline successfully demonstrates that fusing dense semantic segmentation with discrete object detection yields a far more reliable, responsive, and physically realistic navigation system. By relying on direct pixel metrics rather than abstractions, the system achieves lower latency and higher robustness in dynamic environments.
